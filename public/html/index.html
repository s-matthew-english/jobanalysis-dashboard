ABOUT EUROPEAN DATA SCIENCE ACADEMY

The ‘Age of Data’ continues to thrive, with data being produced from all industries at a phenomenal rate that introduces numerous challenges regarding the collection, storage and analysis of this data. To address this problem, the European Data Science Academy (EDSA) will establish a virtuous learning production cycle for Data Science.

The EDSA consortium is a well-balanced European consortium containing 9 partners from 6 European countries (UK, Germany, Sweden, Slovenia, The Netherlands and France). Partners represent universities (OU, KTH, SOUTHAMPTON, TU/e), research institutions (FRAUNHOFER, JSI) and SMEs (ODI, IDEXLAB SAS, Persontyle Limited) covering different forms of organisations.

The European Data Science Academy (EDSA) designs curricula for data science training and data science education across the European Union (EU).

Throughout the project, the curricula and learning resources will be guided and evaluated by experts in both Data Science and pedagogy to ensure they meet the needs of the Data Science community. The sections below outline some of the activities that are being carried out to help meet these goals.

EDSA Dashboard provides Policy Makers, Job Seekers and Experts with a set of analytics options related to Demand Analysis in Data Science domain:

  • a) analyse the required sector specific skillsets for data scientists across the main industrial sectors in Europe;
  • b) view data science trends depending on location and skillset
  • and c) explore data science related materials, courses and videolectures developed by EDSA project.

ABOUT JOB ANALYSIS

Job Analysis allows the user to search the trends of skills requested of a data scientists and job positions that are located in the European Union. It also suggests the learning materials that can be used to learn a specific skill.
